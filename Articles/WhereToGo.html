Best Places to Go Camping in 2023

Determining the "best" camping and hiking spots in the world is subjective and can vary based on personal preferences and interests. However, there are several iconic and renowned locations around the globe that are celebrated for their stunning natural beauty and exceptional outdoor experiences. Here are some of the top camping and hiking spots that are widely acclaimed.


Torres del Paine National Park, Chile

Located in the extreme South of Patagonia, Chile, this location is renowned for its spectacular views, formidable mountain ranges and weather that is as unpredicatable as human nature itself. One who decides to go here would find themselves greeted by small valleys seperated by mesmerising geological features, from glaciers to lakes of the bluest blue.


Banff National Park, Canada

Nestled cosily in the Rockies, Banff national park is a wonderland filled with awesome landscapes that would render anyone speechless. Visitor will be treated with trails leading to glacial lakes, towering forests and unforgiving mountains such as Moraine Lake and Lake Louise. The natural beauty of this spot is so great that it was made Canada's first national park and is part of the Canadian Rocky Mountain Parks UNESCO World Heritage Site.


Yosemite National Park, USA

Located in the Sierra Nevada Mountains in sunny California, Yosemite National Park is known for it's magnificent fjords, giant sequoia trees and iconic landmarks such as Half Dome and El Capitan. This park where waterfalls crash down from the heavens and granite monoliths tower over the ground offers a variety of hiking trails for hikers of any skill level.


Fiordland National Park, New Zealand

As its name may suggest, Fiordland National Park in New Zealand is famous for its glacier-carved fjords of Doubtful and Milford Sound. Three of New Zealand's great walks are found in this park that is known for its hiking and walking opportunities. Besides the breathtaking fjords, Fiordland is also home to lush rainforests and beautiful waterfalls as well as rugged mountains giving way to seas of the purest blue.


The Scottish Highlands, Scotland

The Scotish Highlands are a mountaneous region which enocopmasses the North of -yep, you guessed it- Scotland. It can be called a hiker's paradise due to it's dramatic landscapes and picturesque scenes. The famours Loch Ness lies at its heart, which would be a delight for anyone who has ever had an interest in the mythical Loch Ness Monster, or Nessie as it is fondly known. Other than that the West Highland Way and the Isle of Skye are popular among outdoor enthusiasts.


Conclusion

It is important to keep in mind that these are just a handful of spots in the world and whether or not they are the best is entirely up to you. What can be said though, is that camping and hiking go hand in hand and are two wonderful and satisfying recreational activites that everyone should experience at least once in their lifetime. Regardless of whether you visit one of these spots, or whether you find a place on google, or even if you visit that one little thicket that your grandfather said he went to, camping will always leave you both completely satisfied and wanting more at the same time.
